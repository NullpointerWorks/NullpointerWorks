Introduction

For the majority of people who own a computer treat it as nothing more than a fancy box. A tool that breaks often and is always a hassle to update or kept at reasonable speed. They use it to check their e-mails, view and print documents, update their socials and perhaps play a game of solitair. However, in the eyes of an enthusiast it's not just a fancy box, nor an over-powered calculator. It's a piece of furnature that requires the same love and care like anything else in the household.

In particular the gaming community is expressing this love in a way similar to the people tuning their car's performance and looks. When you search online for water-cooled PCs you find images of hard-tubing cooling systems with colorful coolant and lots of lighting and cable wiring matching the theme.

Mind you I'm not a hardcore PC tuner, but I do enjoy tinkering when I have the oppertunity. One of those opportunities had arisen when I was building my new work PC. It has plenty room for eight fans and since had some multicolor fans laying around, I thought it be fun to have those installed. Unfortunately, the orignal controller for these fans was lacking so I opted to create my own.
